Pricing Risks
COST
4,500 psi exterior SOG is unusually high. Most specs run 3,500–4,000 psi. This mix with 0.45 w/cm + 6% air will cost more. Get supplier confirmation before bid.
COST
40% fly ash substitution in paving (32 1313) is aggressive. Could affect early strength. Confirm mix design can hit 4,000 psi at 28 days in Georgia summer.
COST
Stamped patio color/pattern not yet selected by architect. You're pricing a TBD product. Note that white cement full-depth adds material cost over standard gray.
COST
Joint filling is a return trip. Minimum 1-month deferral after pour; no construction traffic allowed first. Budget a separate mobilization late in schedule.
COST
Cylinder curing box is contractor-furnished. Section 3.16.C.15 — provide "secure, protected curing box." Small but easy to miss.
Schedule Risks
SCHEDULE
FF/FL measurement within 72 hours of each pour. You need a Dipstick crew on-call. Miss the window and the tolerance reading is invalid — potential rework.
SCHEDULE
Stamped patio: 28-day wait before surface treatment. Pour → stamp → cure → wait 28 days → clean → seal. Plan return mobilization.
SCHEDULE
Paving: 14-day traffic exclusion after pour. Active site will need careful sequencing to avoid cutting off access routes.
SCHEDULE
Multiple preinstallation conferences required: vapor barrier (14 days before), CIP concrete, stamped concrete (10 days before), densifier. Budget superintendent time.
SCHEDULE
Stamped patio temp window is 55–80°F. Rules out Georgia summer afternoons and cold winter mornings. Plan pour timing accordingly.
QC / Compliance Risks
QC
Additional testing at contractor's expense if work fails and must be replaced or re-evaluated (§3.16.C.13). Normal testing is Owner-paid; retesting after failure is on us.
QC
Wire fabric must be on chairs — no pulling up. §3.06.F explicitly prohibits it. Inspector will flag this. Brief crew before every pour.
QC
Field-cured cylinders < 85% of lab-cured triggers mandatory investigation and corrective procedures. Watch summer pours.
QC
Stamped installer needs 5 years documented experience + project list submission. Vet your stamped concrete sub early.
GOOD NEWS
$0/day liquidated damages (Contract §5). No daily exposure for late Material Completion. Owner still has actual damages rights, but no preset clock.

Scope Clarifications Needed
RFI 1
Is 09 9673 (Concrete Densifying Sealers) in GC+ scope? It's a Div 09 spec — could be flooring sub. If ours, the 5-year installer qualification and preinstallation conference add cost.
RFI 2
Who is responsible for 03 0516 (Vapor Barrier)? It's a standalone section with shop drawing requirements, manufacturer-specific rebar chairs, and conference requirements. Confirm assignment.
RFI 3
SOG reinforcement — rebar or WWF? Spec lists both products but doesn't specify which for SOG. Structural drawings govern; confirm from S-series sheets before takeoff.
RFI 4
Who produces the SOG joint layout drawing? Required submittal per §1.02.I. Clarify whether GC+ generates this or the structural EOR provides it.
RFI 5
GC+ responsibility for embedded items (sleeves, inserts)? §3.02 covers setting of embedded items but is ambiguous about who is responsible vs. who just places to templates provided by others.
RFI 6
Slab flood testing before epoxy (§3.12.E) — who performs? Required on all epoxy slab areas. Not assigned to a specific trade. Could be us or epoxy contractor.
Design / Spec Ambiguities
RFI 7
Which columns are exposed vs. concealed? Exposed faces require smooth-formed finish + rubbed finish. Column count and finish requirements need to come from architectural drawings — request clarity if not evident.
RFI 8
Stamped patio color + pattern selection. Currently TBD by architect. White cement full-depth is specified, but integral color and release agent color are "architect's selection." Price an allowance or note the open item.
RFI 9
Exterior paving fly ash substitution confirmation. 40% minimum replacement (32 1313 §2.7.B) is high. Verify with ready-mix that a compliant mix can hit 4,000 psi at 28 days in local summer conditions — confirm before bid or note contingency.
RFI 10
Vapor barrier / below-grade waterproofing transition. Shop drawing must show this connection detail, but the waterproofing is a separate spec section. Confirm who owns the interface and provides the detail.
